About the Brain Spectrogram System

A Deep Learning Platform for EEG Signal Analysis and Autism Spectrum Detection

Project Overview

Brain Spectrogram is a cutting-edge EEG analysis system built to process and interpret brainwave data using spectrogram-based deep learning. The system converts EEG signals into time-frequency visual representations and applies neural networks to detect patterns related to visual stimuli and Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD).

It serves both research and clinical applications â€” helping neuroscientists, clinicians, and data scientists gain insights from brain activity efficiently and visually.

Technical Approach
  • EEG signal preprocessing, filtering, and artifact removal
  • Spectrogram generation via Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT)
  • Deep CNN models for visual feature extraction
  • Classification of brain responses and ASD detection
Key Features
  • Patient management and diagnosis history
  • EEG data upload and processing (CSV format)
  • Spectrogram visualization and analysis tracking
  • AI-driven deep learning analysis with confidence scores
  • Exportable PDF reports
Real-World Applications
  • ASD diagnosis support and neurological disorder analysis
  • Neuroscience and brain-computer interface (BCI) research
  • Education and data science research environments
Data Privacy & Ethics
  • Strict data privacy protocols and patient consent
  • HIPAA and GDPR-compliant data storage principles
  • AI decisions are interpretative, not diagnostic
Future Directions
